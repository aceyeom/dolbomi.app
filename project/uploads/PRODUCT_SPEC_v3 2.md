# 멈추지 않는 시간 (The Time That Doesn't Stop)
### Comprehensive Product Specification — v3, rebuilt from the user's emotional reality
> ROK military self-development platform | Working codename: **DOLBOMI** | Jun 2026

---

## PART I — THE EMOTIONAL REBUILD

### The one sentence every soldier says

Search any Korean military forum — 디시 육군 갤러리, 군사 갤러리, brunch, Reddit — and the same sentence appears, written by thousands of soldiers in slightly different words:

> **"까놓고 말하면 1년 6개월 시간낭비하는 거 맞다."**
> *"Let's be honest — it's 18 months of wasted time."*

This is the emotional center of the entire product. Not "level up your stats." Not "compete with your platoon." The real, raw, universal feeling is **the fear that this time is being stolen from you** — that while you stand at a guard post, your friends are interning, dating, building, advancing. The forums call it 뒤처지는 불안 — the anxiety of falling behind.

And the counterweight, also from real soldiers:

> **"목표에 한 걸음씩 다가가고 있다는 것만으로 막연한 불안감을 극복할 수 있다."**
> *"Just knowing you're one step closer to your goal is enough to overcome the vague anxiety."*

> **"뭐든 발전을 했다는 점이 나중에 되어서는 굉장히 부러웠다."**
> *"The ones who developed themselves in any way — later, I envied them so much."*

### The premise, rewritten

The old premise was: *"A gamified RPG where soldiers level up a tactical profile."*

That's a mechanic, not a reason to care. Gamification is the skin. It is not why someone opens the app at 21:00 when they're exhausted.

**The new premise:**

> **DOLBOMI is the proof that your 18 months were not stolen.**
>
> It turns the time the army takes from you into time you took back — and at the end, it hands you the receipt: undeniable evidence of who you became, so you walk out ahead instead of behind.

Everything flows from one promise: **you will not be the person on the forum saying "I wasted 18 months." You will be the person others envy.**

This reframes every feature:
- Stats aren't a game — they're **the proof you're not standing still.**
- The savings tracker isn't a tool — it's **money the army gave you that most soldiers leave on the table.**
- The discharge report isn't a credential — it's **the receipt that says the time counted.**
- The AI companion isn't a chatbot — it's **the one entity that remembers you were trying, on the days no one else saw it.**

### Why this works for all three users at once

The user asked for this to serve all three personas. The "wasted time" frame is the only frame that does, because all three share the same underlying fear:

| Persona | Surface motivation | Shared deep fear | How DOLBOMI answers |
|---------|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| The bored conscript | "I want to look cool / kill time" | "I'm wasting my youth in here" | Makes progress visible and shareable — boredom becomes momentum |
| The anxious soldier | "I'm scared I'm falling behind" | "I'll come out worse than I went in" | Turns formless anxiety into a concrete, daily-shrinking gap |
| The disciplined self-improver | "I want to maximize this time" | "I'll lose my edge / forget my plan" | Gives structure, compounding records, and a path to real-world wins |

The bored one and the disciplined one are not different users. They are the same user on different days. DOLBOMI has to work on the exhausted, demoralized day — which is why the emotional frame, not the game mechanic, has to carry it.

## PART II — THE NAME

"전술 성장" and "Jeonseul Seongjang" are descriptive but emotionally flat — they describe a mechanic (tactical growth). The product needs a name that carries the *time* idea.

### Recommended: **DOLBOMI** (템포)
- Latin/musical root: the rate at which time passes, the pace you set.
- Implies *you* control the rhythm of your own 18 months — the army doesn't.
- Short, English-friendly (works on a global pivot), pronounceable in Korean (템포).
- Tagline: **"군대가 멈춘 시간을, 네 시간으로." (Turn the army's stopped time into your time.)**

### Alternates considered
| Name | Read | Why not (or why maybe) |
|------|------|------------------------|
| 멈추지 않는 시간 | "The time that doesn't stop" | Beautiful, but too long for an app icon label |
| D-Day (디데이) | The countdown everyone already lives by | Too generic, undifferentiated, no ownership |
| 복무일지 | "Service log" | Sounds like a government form |
| 전역각 | Military slang for "discharge is near" | Funny but only resonates with one mood |
| **DOLBOMI** | "Your pace, your time" | **Winner — emotional, short, scalable** |

## PART III — THE STAT SYSTEM, RECONSIDERED

The 6-stat military naming (전투력, 정신력, etc.) is good for immersion but has one flaw for the "all three users" goal: it over-indexes on the military-cosplay fantasy and under-indexes on the **real-world identity** the anxious user is desperate to protect.

The fix: keep the military flavor **in the UI skin**, but map every stat to a **real-life capability the user will carry out the gate.** Each stat answers the question: *"What part of the civilian me am I keeping alive — or building — in here?"*

| Stat (in-app military name) | What it really is | The fear it fights |
|------------------------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| **전투력** Body | Physical fitness, health, sleep | "I'll come out unhealthy / fat / weak" |
| **정신력** Mind | Mental resilience, emotional steadiness, focus | "This place will make me dull / depressed" |
| **자산력** Money | Savings, financial literacy, future capital | "I'll come out broke with nothing saved" |
| **숙련도** Craft | MOS skills, certifications, marketable abilities | "My résumé will have an 18-month hole" |
| **지휘력** People | Leadership, communication, mentoring | "I'll forget how to deal with people / civilians" |
| **담력** Edge | Courage, initiative, comfort-zone expansion | "I'll come out timid, having just obeyed orders" |

This single reframe is the most important change in the rebuild. The stats are no longer a game score. They are **the six pieces of yourself you refuse to let the army take.** The bored user reads them as a cool RPG sheet; the anxious user reads them as a survival checklist; the disciplined user reads them as a development plan. Same six bars.

### The "Edge" stat (담력) is the secret weapon
Most apps reward what you already do. 담력 rewards what you *avoid* — the hard conversation, the certification you keep postponing, the startup competition you're scared to enter. It is the only stat that requires the AI to call you out. It will be the rarest, most-screenshotted, most-bragged-about bar. Make it hard. Make it matter.

## PART IV — THE CORE LOOP, MADE DESIRABLE

A loop is only as good as its weakest, most-tired moment. Here is the loop designed for the 21:00 exhausted day, not the motivated day.

```
  18:00 phone unlock
        │
        ▼
  [60-second check-in]  ──►  "How was today?" (2 taps: mood + energy)
        │                     Not a form. A genuine question from your AI.
        ▼
  [Tonight's 3]  ──►  Three quests, sized to your energy.
        │              Low energy night → all three are 5-min micro-quests.
        │              The app NEVER gives an exhausted soldier a 2-hour quest.
        ▼
  [Do + log]  ──►  Honor system. One tap to complete. Optional photo flex.
        │            Completion animation = the dopamine. Earned, not cheap.
        ▼
  [The bars move]  ──►  You watch a stat bar fill. The gap to your goal shrinks.
        │                 D-day ticks. 적금 number ticks up.
        ▼
  [One line back]  ──►  AI says one thing. Not generic praise.
                         "3일 연속 Body 채웠다. 입대할 때보다 이미 앞서있어."
```

### What makes it desirable (and what killed the old version)

**Old loop problem:** It assumed motivation. It gave you quests and expected you to want them. On a bad day, that's a guilt machine, and guilt machines get deleted.

**New loop principle: the app adapts to your worst day, not your best.** The mood/energy check-in isn't a feature — it's the load-bearing wall. It lets the app say "tonight, just read for 5 minutes, that's a full win" on the day you have nothing left. That single mechanic is the difference between an app you keep and an app you delete in week 3 (when 52% of habit apps lose their users).

## PART V — THE FEATURE SET, REBUILT AND PRIORITIZED

Features are grouped by the emotional job they do, not by technical category. Each is tagged: **[CORE]** = MVP, must ship; **[MOAT]** = hard to copy, build early; **[LATER]** = post-PMF.

### A. "Prove I'm not standing still" — the visibility engine

**A1. The Receipt (전역 리포트, reframed) [MOAT]**
The emotional anchor of the whole app, moved from "a nice export at the end" to **the thing you're building from day one.** From the first day, the user can see a live, growing version of their discharge receipt. Every quest adds to it. The pitch on the download page is literally: *"전역하는 날, 네가 뭘 했는지 증명하는 한 장."* (The one page that proves what you did, on the day you leave.) Final form: radar chart of 6 stats, certs earned, 적금 total, titles, a "before/after" of the future-self profile. Exportable, shareable, beautiful.

**A2. The Gap (전역 후 자아, reframed) [CORE]**
At enlistment, the user writes who they want to be at discharge (job, school, skills, body, savings). The app renders this as a **visible gap** — a second, ghosted set of stat bars showing the target. Every day, the gap between "now me" and "discharge me" shrinks. This converts the formless 뒤처지는 불안 (falling-behind anxiety) into a concrete, daily-shrinking number. This is the single most powerful answer to the anxious user.

**A3. Monthly Wrapped (월간 결산) [CORE]**
Spotify-Wrapped-style shareable card every month. Not annual — monthly, because every month in service deserves proof. "이번 달: 퀘스트 42개 · Body +3 · 적금 +64만 · 새 칭호: 불굴." Built for KakaoTalk group chats. ~18 organic shares per user over a full enlistment.

### B. "The time is becoming money" — the financial engine

**B1. The Money Machine (적금 시뮬레이터) [CORE]**
Already built (see savings simulator widget). The reframe: this is not a calculator, it's **a daily reminder that the army is handing you money most soldiers never collect.** Live on the lockscreen. The projected discharge balance growing every morning is the most emotionally resonant number in the app. Government matching (up to 71%) framed as "공짜 돈" — free money you'd be an idiot to leave behind.

**B2. The ₩120,000 Nobody Spends (자기개발비 네비게이터) [MOAT]**
Every soldier gets ₩120,000/year for self-development. Most never use it. The app surfaces it as quests: "이번 분기 6만원 남음 → 정보처리기능사 응시료로 신청해. 마감 D-12." This is a genuine moat — built on a real government budget that no competitor is activating, with real urgency (deadlines) and real value (free certs).

**B3. Future Capital Quests (재정 리터러시) [CORE]**
Bite-size financial education quests (ETF, compound interest, 청약, 전세 vs 월세). Builds the Money stat. Doubles as a warm-lead funnel to brokerages at discharge. Framed for the user as "전역하고 호구 안 되는 법" (how to not get scammed after discharge).

### C. "I'm building a real future" — the opportunity engine (NEW — the user's key request)

This is the entirely new pillar the user asked for: don't just track self-development, **connect it to real-world opportunities.** This is what makes DOLBOMI more than a habit tracker — it becomes a launchpad.

**C1. Opportunity Radar (기회 레이더) [MOAT]**
A curated, always-current feed of real programs soldiers are eligible for, surfaced based on their stat profile and goals. Built from public data:
- **육군창업경진대회 / 국방 스타트업 챌린지** — the real startup competition pipeline. Connects to 도전! K-스타트업 national finals. **Critically: 포상휴가 (vacation leave) is awarded to soldiers who place** — the single most valuable currency in the army. The app frames it exactly that way: "이 대회 입상 = 포상휴가. 네 아이디어로 휴가 따자."
- 자기개발 자격증 시험 일정 (84 government-subsidized certs)
- 군 e-러닝 / 학점은행제 courses
- 전역 후 채용 연계 프로그램, 청년 지원 정책
The radar doesn't just list these — it generates a **quest path** to qualify for each. "창업대회 D-45. 준비 퀘스트: ① 아이디어 한 줄 정리 ② 시장 조사 30분 ③ 팀원 1명 찾기."

**C2. The Vacation Engine (포상휴가 트래커) [CORE]**
Reframes the entire app around the army's real reward currency. Many achievements in service grant 포상휴가 — competitions, 표창, 체력왕, certain certs. The app aggregates every leave-earning opportunity and tracks the user's progress toward extra days off. **For the bored/cynical user who doesn't care about "growth," this is the hook that doesn't sound like self-help — it sounds like beating the system to get more time at home.** This single feature makes the app desirable to the user who'd otherwise roll their eyes at "level up your mind."

**C3. Startup Sidecar (창업 사이드카) [LATER]**
For the entrepreneurially-inclined: a lightweight idea journal + the disconfirmation-first evaluation framework, tied directly to the 육군창업경진대회 submission timeline. Turns "I have a business idea" into "I have a submission-ready deck and a shot at 포상휴가 + the national K-Startup pipeline." A natural premium upsell, and a genuine differentiator no military app touches.

### D. "Something here actually understands me" — the intelligence engine

**D1. The One Who Remembers (전술 AI 동반자) [MOAT]**
Not a chatbot. A persistent character with memory of your last 14 days. Persona: a 말년 병장 / dry veteran NCO who's seen it all — direct, funny, allergic to excuses, but quietly on your side. The emotional job, drawn straight from the forums where soldiers describe how invisible their effort feels: **it's the one entity that noticed you were trying on the days no one else did.** It reads your journal before it speaks. It calls out avoidance ("Edge 스탯 2주째 그대로네. 뭐가 무서워?"). It celebrates the unglamorous wins. Premium.

**D2. Sunday Letter (감정 결산) [MOAT]**
Every Sunday, a private 3-paragraph reflection generated from the week's journal: your dominant mood, what you actually improved, what you dodged. Framed as 자기 객관화 (seeing yourself clearly), not therapy. This matters enormously given that **22% of ROK soldiers are officially classified as 관심병사** — this gives the silent majority a private mental check-in with zero stigma and no chain-of-command visibility.

**D3. Adaptive Quests (난이도 자동 조정) [CORE]**
The engine that makes the core loop survive bad days. Tracks completion per stat over 7 days; shrinks difficulty when you're struggling, escalates when you're cruising. Mood check-in feeds it. This is invisible to the user but it's why they don't quit.

### E. "I'm not alone in here" — the social engine, de-risked

The old plan's "collective punishment / 부대 전투력" mechanic is **cut entirely.** It backfires (the slacker just deletes the app), it's politically dangerous inside a chain of command, and it makes the app feel like more army. Replaced with opt-in, positive-sum social.

**E1. Squad (분대) — opt-in only [CORE]**
You and up to 7 buddies *choose* to form a squad. Shared challenges, shared wins, a squad banner. No punishment if someone falls behind — the others just keep going. Mirrors how real barracks friendships actually work (the forums are clear: good 동기 relationships are the #1 thing that makes service bearable).

**E2. Anonymous Wall (익명 고충 벽) [CORE]**
A feed where soldiers post one anonymous line about a hard day. Others react with a single 🫡-style solidarity tap. No names, no comments, no unit info. A pressure valve with zero consequences. Directly serves the mental-health reality without being a scary "hotline."

**E3. Rival (라이벌) [LATER]**
Optional 1:1 friendly rivalry with one other soldier (a real friend, or a matched stranger at your level). A 72-hour XP race, low-stakes, rematchable. Duolingo-style. Drives word-of-mouth without the toxicity of unit-wide leaderboards.

**E4. Mentor (선임 멘토) [LATER]**
병장-tier users can send one daily quest suggestion to a junior they're paired with; both earn when the junior completes it. Models healthy 선임-후임 dynamics. Appears on both profiles as a positive record.

### F. The skin — "this looks cool enough to show people"

For the bored user, aesthetics ARE the feature. The 취사병 전설이 되다 status-window look is the visual hook.

**F1. The HUD Lockscreen Widget [CORE]** — rank badge, tonight's quest, D-day, streak, 적금 number, 6 stat pips. The status window from the drama, on your lockscreen. Glanceable in 2 seconds.
**F2. Skill Tree (스킬 트리) [LATER]** — each stat branches into a visible 3-tier tree. The most screenshot-worthy surface. Coveted, not just decorative.
**F3. Titles (칭호) [CORE]** — "철벽," "정보처리 장인," "심연의 담력" (rarest). Identity anchors you display under your name.
**F4. Completion animation [CORE]** — the Solo-Leveling level-up moment. The dopamine. Make it feel earned.

## PART VI — INTERACTIVE ELEMENTS (what the user touches)

Ranked by how often a user touches them and how much desirability they create:

1. **The 60-second check-in** (daily, highest-frequency) — two taps, feels like care not work.
2. **Quest completion tap + animation** (3×/day) — the core dopamine delivery.
3. **The Money Machine slider** (weekly) — drag deposit/months, watch the discharge number move. Genuinely fun to play with; it's hope made interactive.
4. **The Gap bars** (daily glance) — watching "discharge me" and "now me" converge.
5. **Monthly Wrapped reveal** (monthly) — the share moment, the anticipation spike.
6. **Opportunity Radar browse** (weekly) — "wait, I could win vacation for this?"
7. **AI conversation** (as needed) — the depth moment, the reason it feels alive.

## PART VII — MONETIZATION, ALIGNED WITH THE NEW FRAME

The frame is "we prove your time counted." Money should never feel like it's taxing that promise. So: **the proof is always free. The acceleration is paid.**

| Line | Timing | What's paid | Why it doesn't break trust |
|------|--------|-------------|----------------------------|
| **DOLBOMI+ subscription** ₩4,900/mo | During service | AI companion (unlimited), Sunday Letter, 5-min library, adaptive coaching | The intelligence is the premium; the tracking is free |
| **Cert prep affiliate** | During service | Links to 에듀윌/해커스 courses, reimbursed via the ₩120K gov budget | User spends government money, not their own |
| **Sponsored quests** | During service | Brands fund quest tracks w/ real rewards (CU 상품권, etc.) | Soldier gets real value; it's a gift, not an ad |
| **Discharge Pack** ₩29,000 | At D-30 (one-time) | Full Receipt PDF + résumé templates + 30-day post-discharge quest pack | Sold at peak motivation, to people who got real value |
| **Brokerage CPA** | At discharge | Toss/Kiwoom/KB pay per account opened | 18 months of literacy quests = a genuinely warm lead |
| **B2B Receipt access** | Post-discharge | Recruiters pay to access verified skill records | The Receipt is a real credential, ethically sold w/ consent |

**Critical principle:** the free tier must be genuinely enough to fulfill the core promise. A soldier who never pays a won should still walk out the gate with a Receipt that proves their time counted. Paid only makes the journey faster, smarter, and more accompanied. This is what keeps the brand trustworthy with a cohort that has very little disposable income and a finely-tuned radar for being exploited.

## PART VIII — THE BRUTAL RISKS (unchanged by good design)

A rebuild doesn't erase the structural threats. Honest accounting:

1. **군돌이 copies it.** They have 60만 MAU (90%+ penetration) and just raised. They could bolt a quest layer onto their utility app in 3 months. **Defense:** the emotional frame + the Receipt + 18-month longitudinal data + the Opportunity/Vacation engine are the hard-to-copy moat. The skin is not. Build the moat first, fast. Realistic window: 12–18 months.

2. **The LLM quest quality is the product.** If quests feel templated by day 5, the emotional frame can't save it. **Defense:** the journal-to-quest pipeline and the AI companion's memory are where all engineering effort goes. This is the bet. Test it with real soldiers (you have a built-in test group) before scaling.

3. **No verified-data API exists.** 밀리패스/국방 systems are walled; you cannot verify rank/MOS/fitness without an 18-month government partnership. **Defense:** honor system is fine for a *self*-development app (Habitica, Finch prove it — false data hurts only the user). The Receipt is honestly framed as "self-reported growth record," and the *verifiable* parts (certs, 적금) carry the credibility weight.

4. **Discharge cliff.** Every user churns at 전역. LTV is structurally capped at ~18–21 months. **Defense:** the 예비역 alumni layer + the Receipt's post-discharge career value extend the relationship. But accept this is a high-throughput, seasonal-cohort business, not sticky SaaS — and price/monetize for the discharge moment accordingly.

5. **Policy risk.** App bans on military networks, the expanding-then-contracting phone-hours pilot, brokerage/financial-product licensing for any 적금/주식 reward. **Defense:** stay a personal-time consumer app (never a "military network" app), keep rewards as third-party 상품권 not financial products, and watch the phone-hours policy (currently expanding, 2025 pilot).
